### Banked development

Development rights that were previously assigned to a parcel, but the structure was removed (typically as part of demolition and site restoration). The right is held in reserve and can be used to rebuild on the same site or transferred to another parcel for new construction.

Banked rights don't count toward on-the-ground totals — there's no physical structure standing — but they DO count against the Regional Plan's cumulative cap, since they remain authorized.
