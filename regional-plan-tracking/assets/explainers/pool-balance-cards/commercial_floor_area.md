### What's a commercial floor area allocation?

Under the Tahoe Regional Planning Agency (TRPA) [Code of Ordinances](https://www.trpa.gov/code-of-ordinances/), commercial floor area (CFA) is the development right required for buildings that are designated for commercial use under a permit. Commercial floor area includes the gross square footage of floor area within the outer wall of a commercial building, not including stairwells and airshafts, parking areas, driveways, or walkways.

The Regional Plan caps the cumulative total of additional commercial floor area at **1,000,000 square feet** (800,000 from the 1987 Regional Plan and 200,000 from the 2012 Regional Plan). Additional CFA is assigned based on section 50.6 of the TRPA Code.

CFA is assigned to local jurisdiction pools associated with area plans and community plans. TRPA and local jurisdictions distribute additional commercial floor area by taking into consideration such factors as demonstrated need, the expected ability to achieve or maintain environmental thresholds, compatibility with other community plans, and other relevant factors. TRPA also maintains a special projects pool of commercial floor area to promote major projects that result in the construction of threshold-related environmental improvements, to promote transfer of development that results in substantial environmental benefits, and to rehabilitate substandard development.

Current balances for commercial floor area are shown in the [Pool Balance Report](https://parcels.laketahoeinfo.org/DevelopmentRightPool/DevelopmentRightPoolBalanceReport).
