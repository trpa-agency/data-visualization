### What's a residential allocation?

Under the Tahoe Regional Planning Agency (TRPA) [Code of Ordinances section 90.2](https://www.trpa.gov/code-of-ordinances/), a residential allocation is an apportionment of additional development, providing the opportunity to build one new residential unit in the Tahoe Basin. To construct a new residential dwelling, the property must have a potential residential unit of use (the ability to develop a lot) and obtain a residential allocation from the respective local jurisdiction or TRPA.

The Regional Plan caps the cumulative total at **8,687 residential allocations** (6,087 from the 1987 Regional Plan and 2,600 from the 2012 Regional Plan). Allocations are metered out to local jurisdictions, releasing 130 each year, to pace development with environmental capacity.

Each jurisdiction distributes allocations from their pool as permits are approved. Unused allocations are returned to the pool. When a pool runs out, property owners wanting to develop must wait for the next round of allocations, transfer or convert other development rights, or receive a bonus unit. See the [TRPA Development Rights program](https://www.trpa.gov/development-rights/) for more information.

Current pool balances are shown in the [Pool Balance Report](https://parcels.laketahoeinfo.org/DevelopmentRightPool/DevelopmentRightPoolBalanceReport).
