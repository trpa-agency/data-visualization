### What's a tourist bonus unit?

Under the Tahoe Regional Planning Agency (TRPA) [Code of Ordinances](https://www.trpa.gov/code-of-ordinances/), tourist accommodation units (TAUs) are overnight units, with one or more bedrooms and with or without cooking facilities, primarily designed to be rented by the day or week and occupied on a temporary basis by persons whose permanent residence is elsewhere.

The Regional Plan caps the cumulative total of additional tourist accommodation units at **400 units** (all 400 from the 1987 Regional Plan; no additional units were added in the 2012 Regional Plan). Additional TAUs are assigned based on section 50.7 of the TRPA Code.

Some TAUs are assigned to local jurisdiction pools associated with area plans and community plans. TRPA also maintains a pool of TAUs to promote major projects that result in the construction of threshold-related environmental improvements, to promote transfer of development that results in substantial environmental benefits, and to rehabilitate substandard development.

Current balances for tourist accommodation units are shown in the [Pool Balance Report](https://parcels.laketahoeinfo.org/DevelopmentRightPool/DevelopmentRightPoolBalanceReport).
